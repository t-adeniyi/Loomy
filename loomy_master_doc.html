01

Product Overview

One-liner

Loomy is a platform that helps creatives turn scattered inspiration into structured creative development.

The Problem

What users experience today

  • Save thousands of visual references but can't find them when needed
  • Inspiration stays scattered across platforms with no organization
  • Zero structure to bridge inspiration and action
  • Current tools built for scrolling, not studying or building
  • Creators go uncredited across millions of saved images

The cycle that Loomy breaks

1

Motivated
3
Nothing happens — the gap Loomy closes

The gap between inspiration and action is the core problem. Not lack of discipline — lack of the right tools.

Target users

Who Loomy is built for

Artists, fashion enthusiasts, interior designers, brand builders, and anyone who is endlessly inspired but perpetually stuck. The common thread: people who save inspiration constantly but never convert it into creative output. Not just visual artists — anyone with a creative practice that involves collecting references and building toward something.

Key differentiator

Inspiration → Action workflow Human-made content only Creator attribution built-in Context travels with every save Not just a save button — a workspace

🧵

Threads

The atomic unit of Loomy. Any saved piece of content — an image, a link, an upload — is a Thread. Threads carry context with them: creator attribution, tags, and any notes added.

Browse the Explore page → save anything → it becomes a Thread in your Collection.

📁

Collections

Organized groups of Threads. Users can build, browse, and filter Collections by tag, theme, or project. Works as a standalone tool — no Loom required for casual or quick use cases.

File folders for creative assets: lyric sheets, premade boards, outfit saves, reference packs.

🪡

Looms

The action workspace. Pull Threads from Collections, add notes, columns, and structure. Build workflows: an art study with techniques, a capsule wardrobe with links, a room makeover checklist.

Looms can contain Looms (a "Loom within a Loom" for practice boards). Supports progress tracking and sketch uploads.

👤

Attribution

Auto-identifies the original creator on upload. Where automation fails, the community fills the gap. Proper credit travels with every Thread across the entire platform.

Knowing who inspires you is part of knowing who you are creatively. Attribution is a core value, not an add-on.

Explore page

Discovery surface

  • Visual discovery feed — similar to Pinterest's explore but purpose-built for creatives
  • Human-made content only — AI-generated content is excluded or clearly labeled (policy TBD, see feedback)
  • Interest-based personalization — users pick creative interests at onboarding to shape their feed immediately
  • Search by technique, style, medium, or artist name

User Personas

Two core personas developed from an affinity map of initial user feedback (no mockup). Both have high motivation for inspiration — they differ in how they process and act on it. Full detailed personas are in loomy_personas_v2.html.

✦

The Dreamer

Sees inspiration everywhere. Struggles to finish.

About them

Spontaneous and feeling-led. Inspiration hits constantly but momentum dies between spark and execution. Works in creative bursts, thrives in flow state. The problem isn't finding ideas — it's holding onto them long enough to act.

Sources of inspiration

  • Pinterest (passive, frequent, 2am scrolling)
  • YouTube (side-by-side with Procreate in real time)
  • The world around them — everything is potential inspiration

What they need from Loomy

  • Finished work to reverse-engineer, not step-by-step tutorials
  • Human-made only — AI art filtered out
  • Theme-grouped saves (mood/concept, not just chronology)
  • Real-time accessibility — reachable mid-flow
  • Spacious, uncluttered UI — friction kills the mood

Loyalty

Inspiration

High

Love for Loomy

Medium

Affinity depends on whether Loomy preserves flow. Won't tolerate friction or clutter.

◈

The Planner

Researches before creating. Needs structure.

About them

Process-driven and detail-oriented. Never begins without a plan. Deeply cares about creative identity — wants to know who made something and why, not just what it looks like. Needs a workspace, not just a save button.

Sources of inspiration

  • Artist research — follows specific creators as identity anchors
  • Curated mood boards — builds boards actively, not passively
  • Unexpected places — museums, streets, books — then organizes intentionally

What they need from Loomy

  • Contextual saves — artist credit, notes, and context attached to everything
  • Structured project folders (lyric sheets, premade boards, asset groups)
  • Taggable, filterable content — browsing without filters is useless
  • Cloud-native storage — no downloading to device
  • Full creative workspace, not a collection tool

Loyalty

Most likely to become a power user. Loomy directly addresses their core frustrations around organization, context, and storage.

04

Scenarios

Two user scenarios grounding the platform in real-world use. The first shows Loomy's deepest workflow (Threads → Collections → Loom). The second shows Collections-only use, demonstrating versatility for casual users who don't need a Loom every time.

S1

Learning to Draw from References

Charlie — Neuroscience major + Drawing minor, aspiring comic artist

Charlie needs to learn how to draw heads at any angle. She starts on the Loomy Explore page, searching "head angles" and curating reference Threads into her "Anatomy Studies" Collection. She then opens a new Loom, pulls 15 Threads and organizes them by view (front, ¾, profile). As she practices, she adds notes on techniques she liked and uploads her sketches to track progress. She has a living reference system she can return to anytime.

Explore → Thread → Collection → Loom Full workflow Progress tracking Skill development
S2

Figuring Out What to Wear on a Date

Tyler — 22-year-old post-grad, casual user introduced by a roommate

Tyler has a date the next night and doesn't know what to wear. He opens Loomy (first time, roommate's suggestion), searches "men's casual outfits" and "date fits," and saves 8 outfit Threads to his "clothes." Collection. He opens the Collection and cross-checks with what he actually has in his closet, narrowing to 3 realistic options. He screenshots them and texts his roommate asking "which one?" Roommate votes for option 2. Tyler picks it and heads out. He solved his problem without ever needing to open a Loom.

Explore → Thread → Collection only Casual / low-stakes No Loom required Versatility proof

Key competitive insight

Pinterest is the most common comparison reviewers make — and the distinction needs to be made clearer upfront. Loomy's core differentiators are the Loom workspace (structured action), automatic attribution, and skill development tools — things Pinterest doesn't do at all.
